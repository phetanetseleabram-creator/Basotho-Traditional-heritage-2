MUSIC and DANCE OF THE BASOTHO PEOPLE

The Basotho people of Lesotho have rich cultural heritage,with traditional music and dance playing a significant role in their daily lives and celebrations.

Traditional Basotho Music

Basotho traditional music is characterised by the use of instruments such as:

  • Djembe: A rope-turned goblet drum originally from west africa,used in variuos cultural celebrations.
  • Moropa:A traditional drum used in basotho music and dance.
  • Setolo-tolo: A traditional instrument used to create rhythmic sounds.
  • Segankuru:A tradional stringed instrument,similar to a guitar or harp.
  • Thomo: A traditional bow instrument,played by plucking the strings.
  • Khati:A traditional instrument,similar to a flute or whistle
  • Sekhankula:A traditional instrumenr used to create rhythmic sounds and accompaniment.
  • Lekolulo:A traditional pipe or whistle instrument.
  • Lesiba:This unique instrument,also known as a Sticker cipher is a wind and string instrument played by herding boys.
  • Lekhitlane and Lenaka: Thes are traditional horns used for spiritual purposes by trditional doctors and mediums
  • Sekupu: SA traditional drum used in spititual ceremonies.
  • Merthloane:Ankle rattles worn by dancers to provide rhythmic accompaniment during performance.
