THIS IS THE BASOTHO'S CULTURAL ATTIRE

1.SEANA MARENA : A prestigious,high-quality wool balnket worn for formal occations.
2.MOHOBELO BLANKET: A fertility blanket wotrn by boys before their initiation ceremony.
3.LEKHOLO BLANKET: A blanket worn by boys after completing their initiation ceremonynto manhood.
4.MOTLOTLEHI BLANKET: A special blanket worn by a man on his wedding day.
5.KOBO EA MOTLOTLEHI : The king's blanket, worn during his coronation.
6.LEHLOSI BLANKET: Made from the skin of the leopard or other wild cat, historically reserved for chiefs.
7.VICTORIA ENGLAND BLANKET: A blanket with symbolic patterns honoring Queen Victoria, given to King Lerotholi in 1897.
8.MORENA BLANKET : An everyday blanket ,often with maize motifs symbolizing prosperity.
9.SEFATE BLANKET: An everyday blanket featuring a tree motif.
10.POONE BLANKET A blanket with a prominent maize pattern , representing reproduction and wealth.
11.TSEHA :An undergarment made of sheepskin, traditionally worn by young boys to cover their private parts.
12.LITOPO: Large rain boots worn by herd boys for protection against the wet, muddy mountain terrain. .
13.MOKOROTLO:A conical woven straw hat, made from moseha grass, which is the national symbol of Lesotho.
14.KUOANE:A bonnet-type hat made wildcat skin, typically worn by younger men.
15.SEKOLA:An animal skin hat traditionally worn by warriors.
16.LIETA TSA LETLALO: Traditional leather sandal made from cowhide,suited for the rugged terrain.
17.LEHLOSI(LEOPARD SKIN): A ceremonial blanket for chiefs,made from leopard skin.
18.SESHOESHOE:A brightly coloured,printed cotton fabric used for makig long dresses, skirts,and other garmants for special occations.
19.THEPA:A wraparound skirt or apron made from a colorful or woven material,worn over a seshoeshoe dress for domestic or rituals.
20.THETHANA:A dress made of clay beads,traditionally worn by young girls.
21.SEFAHA SA LETSOPA: beaded neckpiece made of clay,worn by young girls.
22.MOSE OA LEKOKO:A skirt made of woven fiber or cow skin,worn by girls after coming of age.
23.TUKU:A headscarf worn by women,tied in intricate styles to signify respect.modesty,or marital status.
24.BEADED NECKLASES AND WAIST BEADS:Traditional accesories handmade with patterns that hoild cultural significance.
25.BASOTHO BLANKET:Worn wrapped around the waist or upper body,sometimes with a headscarf.
26.KOBO E NT'SO:A plain black cloth wrapped around the shoulders for mourning or remamberance ceremonies.
27.LINGOETSI: A special blanket given to a bride for her trosseau.
28.SEROKOLO:A hide garment which is part of the initiation regalia.
29.MOTLATSI BLANKET:A blanket produced to pay tribute to the birth of a prince.
30.INITIATION SCHOOL CLOTHING:Special garments are worn during initiation rites(lebollo) for both boys and girls.
31.CEREMONIAL BLANKETS:Blankets with special designs,such as the Jibilee ea Boipuso,are used to commemorate significant national events.
32.KHARETSA:A design named for the aloe plant,which is indigenious to the Maloti Mountains.
